# Seasonal variations in glacier velocity in the High Mountain Asia region during 2015–2020

-	Study of seasonal variations in glacier velocity in the High Mountain Asia region (HMA).
-	Average glacier velocity: 0.053 md-1
-	Fastest velocity when glaciers had a high area and slope.
-	Fastest flow in early summer / spring (0.058 md-1)
-	Followed by summer (0.050), autumn (0.041) and winter (0.040)
-	Zhang et al., (2023) compares results with other studies in HMA – various results which show various glacier velocities:
-	Nyainqêntanglha Mountain glaciers show faster velocity in downstream parts of the glaciers in summer but in the winter the downstream parts flowed faster (Zhang et al., 2020).
-	Other studies which find faster flows in summer: Sam et al. (2018) – Himalayas
-	Studies which found faster flows in winter: Fu et al. (2021) – Dagongba Glacier; Yang et al. (2020) – Parlung No4. Glacier.
-	Zhang et al. (2023) analyses the monthly resolution of all of these studies – none distinguish between early summer and late summer – finds that all flow fastest in the early summer.
-	These results are consistent with the notion of a high magnitude of meltwater input into the drainage system in late spring / early summer causing an acceleration in glacier velocity prior to the development of an efficient, channelised subglacial drainage system which is capable of discharging large volumes of meltwater, causing a slow down over the summer before reverting back to a distributed system in the winter (Yang et al., 2022)
Potentially helpful sidenotes:
-	Study finds that the flow velocity in the ablation areas of glaciers terminating in proglacial lakes is higher than those of land-terminating glaciers.
-	Ice thickness comes into play:
-	Lake-terminating glaciers have thicker ice because they do not experience the gradual thinning of the glacier towards its terminus as the presence of proglacial lakes creates an abrupt end at a calving cliff.
-	The thickness of ice drives its flow and therefore the difference in the terminus of the glacier can be said to be a factor influencing glacier velocity (Pronk et al., 2021)
