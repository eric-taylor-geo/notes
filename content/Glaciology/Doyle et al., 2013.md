#  Ice tectonic deformation during the rapid in situ drainage of a supraglacial lake on the Greenland Ice Sheet

-              Russell Glacier

-              Lake F – initially drains slowly by overflow then rapidly by hydrofracturing

-              4 GPS and 6 seismometers record complex pattern of dynamic response

![[Pasted image 20241108143149.png]]
- Main fracture in the orientation which the ice is moving (right to left)
- Velocity and uplift response suggests 3 phases:
1. Initial drainage without much transverse extension
2. Fracture opening with dominant transverse extension
3. Fracture closure

![[Pasted image 20241108143241.png]]
- Like [[Das et al., 2008]], GPS is shunted due to hydrofracture
- Up to a meter of uplift associated with water reaching the bed – water fills a ‘blister’ on the bed. Jacks up the ice temporarily
