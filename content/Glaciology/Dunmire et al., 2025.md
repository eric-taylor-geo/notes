# Greenland Ice Sheet Wide Supraglacial Lake Evolution and Dynamics: Insights From the 2018 and 2019 Melt Seasons

From SPRI notes:

- Automatically classify lakes using machine learning algorithms which recognise common temporal patterns to make each classification.
- Four catagories of lake behavior:
	- Refreeze
	- Buried
	- Rapid drain
	- Slow drainage
- 2019 vs 2018 difference:
	- Change in the proportion of drainage and refreezing of lakes
	- Less refreezing in 2019
	- Evidence of higher elevaion rapid drainage events up to 1898 m, previous studies suggested 1600 m was an upper boundary
	- We would expect warmer years to have a greater lake depth due to more melt, however 2018 lakes were deeper than in 2019 despite being a colder melt year.
		- Lakes drained sooner in 2019 preventing growth.
		- Warmer temperatures led to **greater surface meltwater production**, contributing to more frequent and earlier lake drainage, particularly rapid drainage events.

Reasons for difference:

- In 2018 (a low melt year), many lakes refroze, but in 2019, a significant number of those same lakes drained, especially slowly.
- Slow drainage increased from 26% to 40% across the GrIS between 2018 and 2019.
- Warmer conditions in 2019 exposed crevasses earlier, permitting hydrofracture and multi-lake drainage through cascades (e.g., [[Stevens et al., 2024]])
- Rapidly draining lakes were observed at elevations above 1800 m, challenging previous assumptions about an upper hydrofracture limit of 1600 m.
- Lakes that drained in 2019 were sometimes found in depressions where larger, non-draining lakes had formed in 2018, implying that prior drainage events can influence future meltwater routing and lake dynamics.
