# Seasonal variations in Greenland Ice Sheet motion: Inland extent and behaviour at higher elevations


## Abstract

- GPS observations along a transect on west Greenland Ice Sheet margin.
- In situ measurements of air temp, surface ablation and satellite measurements of albedo and supraglacial lake drainage are used to investigate the hydrological controls on ice velocity changes
- Finds strong positive correlation between rates of annual ablation and changes in annual ice motion - sites nearest the margin show the greatest annual variations in ice motion (15-18%) - those above 1000 m elevation show less (3-8%)

**Cryo-hydrological warming**
- Heat conduction from water within the englacial system causes ice temperatures to be raised [[Philips et al., 2010]]
- Raised temperatures reduce ice viscosity, contributing to faster ice flow and deformation

## Conclusions

- Seasonal changes in horizontal ice velocity on a land-terminating portion of the western Greenland Ice Sheet are forced by the generation of surface meltwater which is able to reach the ice-bed interface
- Velocity variations propagate inland as higher elevations are exposed to increased surface melting
	- Seasonal melting of the Greenland Ice Sheet is expected to extend over a wider area and be more prolonged [[Hanna et al., 2008]]
- Patterns in the timing and rate of meltwater delivery to the ice-bed interface are key controls on the magnitude on hydrologically-forced velocity variations
