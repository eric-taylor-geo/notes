# Projections of Peak Water Timing from the East Rongbuk Glacier, Mt Everest, Using a Higher-Order Ice Flow Model

Related information:

- In recent decades, Mt. Everest experienced an atmospheric warming trend of approximately 0.033 K a−1, and glaciers in the region are shrinking (Kang et al., 2022)
- Uses elevation differences to infer surface changes
- Velocity data gathered using a feature-tracking approach - Robin-inversion algorithm
- Glacier geometry from modelling and GPR
- East rongbuk has been relatively well-surveyed in situ glaciological data
- From our prognostic experiments, we find ERG will continuously loss mass this century. For the SSP-126 scenario, ERG may likely produce peak annual meltwater runoff at 2030, while for SSP-370 and SSP-585, the peak meltwater runoff timing is around at 2060.
