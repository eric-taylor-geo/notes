# Glacier mass changes in Rongbuk catchment on Mt. Qomolangma from 1974 to 2006 based on topographic maps and ALOS PRISM data

## Abstract

- Tracking elevation changes in glacier surfaces in the Rongbuk Catchment through the comparison of DEMs
- Average elevation change of glacier surface between 1974 and 2006 was -0.47 ± 0.23 m a^-1
- Debris-covered ice thinned much more rapidly than the exposed ice
##  Study Area

- Rongbuk Catchment (RC) covers an area of 280 km2 ranging from 5120 m to 8844 m a.s.l.
- Rongbuk River (RR) is mainly fed by Rongbuk Glacier (RG, hereafter), which is the largest glacier in RC and has been fed by three major individual branches: the East, Middle, and West RGs
- The debris-covered tongues of the three branches had once formed a joint tongue, while they have been separated from each other later
- The exact glacier terminus is difficult to locate due to the debris cover (TSETCAS, 1975, Shi and Cheng, 1991).
- The thickness of debris cover varies from a few centimeters to several meters

## Discussion

**Glacier thinning and debris cover conditions**

- Assumed that bedrock elevation is unchanged
- Thickening or thinning at a point on a glacier is caused by the surface mass balance and the flow velocity caused by longitudinal compression of the glacier
- Despite debris-cover being known to insulate sub-debris ice, debris-coveted portions of the RG still had the highest rates of ablation - owing to being at a lower altitude 
	- Thus, whilst they may have reduced the rate of melt than if the glacier had a clean-ice tongue, debris-covered portions still displayed greater surface lowering than clean-ice regions up-glacier

**Possible climatic controls**

- Rising temperatures as a major factor for glacier recession and increase in runoff [[Liu et al., 2010]]
- Increased black carbon concentrations between 1975-2000 lowered albedo [[Kaspari et al., 2011]]
- Weakening of summer Indian monsoon has reduced local precipitation [[Yang et al., 2011]] - decreased snow accumulation on East RG [[Kaspari et al., 2008]]
