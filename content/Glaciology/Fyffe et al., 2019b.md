# Do debris-covered glaciers demonstrate distinctive hydrological behaviour compared to clean glaciers?

**Summary from [[Miles et al., 2020]]**
- Study of 33 dye tracing return curves on Miage Glacier
- Upglacier ice is covered by a thin debris layer - during the melt season it produced an efficient drainage system to the terminus
- The heavily debris-covered lower ablation area restricted the development of an efficient subsurface system. The drainage system of the lower ablation area instead flowed into the efficient system driven from upglacier.

# Abstract

- Supraglacial debris influences the distribution of glacier surface melt -> melt drives the evolution of glacial drainage systems - thus, debris-covered glaciers should have differing drainage systems than those of clean-glaciers.
