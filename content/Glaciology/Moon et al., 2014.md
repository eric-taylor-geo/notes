# Distinct patterns of seasonal Greenland glacier velocity

- Present a 5-year record of seasonal velocity measurements for 55 marine-terminating glaciers
- Classify three types of behaviour for marine-terminating glaciers
	- Type 1: Seasonal speed variations are driven by the position of the terminus
	- Type 2: Seasonal changes are correlated to the volume of meltwater runoff in the catchment
	- Type 3: Seasonal speeds relate to runoff volumes, but their speeds show an initial maxima in the early melt season and then decline due to the adaptation of the subglacial hydrological system to high runoff.
- Type 1 are terminus driven, type 2 are runoff driven, and type 3 are runoff adapting.
- Glacier type varies in time and space (ofc it does)
- E.g., an exceptionally strong melt year may cause a Type 1 glacier to become type 2 or 3
- Type 1 (terminus-driven): 6 glaciers
- Type 2 (runoff-driven): 15 glaciers
- Type 3 (runoff-adapting): 12 glaciers
