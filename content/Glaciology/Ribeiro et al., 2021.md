# Warm modified circumpolar deep water intrusions drive ice shelf melt and inhibit dense shelf water formation in Vincennes Bay, East Antarctica 

- Antarctic Bottom Water (AABW) production supplies the deep limb of the global overturning circulation
- Weddell and Ross Seas are key sites for AABW production
- Additional sources of AABW production have been discovered around EA - Vincennes Bay
- Study uses oceanographic observations of Vincennes' continental shelf using conductivity, temperature, and depth (CDT) data from elephant seals (wtf that is SO cool)
- Finds that Vincennes Bay has EA's warmest recorded intrusions of mCDW and that mCDW drives basal melt under the Vanderford and Underwood ice shelves
- Study also provides the first record of the inflow of meltwater into the region
- Meltwater increases the stratification and hinders the formation of Dense Shelf Water which in turn affects AABW production
- Glaciers drain the Aurora Basin - which has already been highlighted as vulnerable to MISI 
	- [[Miles et al., 2016]] https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.1501350 [[Pelle et al., 2020]] https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1029/2019GL086821
- Aurora basin holds 7 m of SLR equivalent 
- Results therefore highlight the vulnerability of East Antarctic Ice Sheet to intrusions of mCDW
