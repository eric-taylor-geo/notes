# Retreat of Humboldt Gletscher, North Greenland, Driven by Undercutting From a Warmer Ocean

*bruh this guy has written EVERYTHING*

- Between 1972-2019, Humboldt Gletscher lost 161 Gt of ice, the fourth largest contribution from Greenland's outlet glaciers
- Humboldt is the widest glacier in Greenland at 100 km wide ([[Weidick, 1995]]), with a slow motion (100 m yr-1)
- It has 19 cm of SLR potential
- A submarine channel extends into the interior in the north ([[Morlighem et al., 2017]])
- The enhanced intrusion of warm Atlantic Intermediate Water in the fjords in the 1990s is proposed to have melted the glacier fronts, destabilising them and increasing mass discharge ([[Christoffersen et al., 2011]]).
- A lack of ocean and depth measurements makes it difficult to quantify water temperatures within the fjords, limiting the interpretation of changes.
- Use multibeam echo sounder to collect bathymetry data
- Airborne Conductivity Temperature Depth (AXCTD) data were collected to estimate thermal forcing.
- Runoff reconstructed using RACMO
- Humboldt had a small ice tongue (27 km2 - compared to a 100 km wide tongue, this is minuscule) which disappeared between 1990 and 2016. The study assumes that the terminus following the ice tongue's loss is the same as the grounding line, and does not consider any buttressing effects due to its small size.

## Results and discussion

- 1.7 degrees C warming since 1948, the rate of which is accelerating.
- Between 1962 and 2019 it retreated by 3.4 km.
- Runoff correlates strongly with warming.
- Warm Atlantic Intermediate Water is intruding
- Ice thinning only explains 30% of the retreat signal
- Enhanced surface melt contributes 43% of the thinning, and thus only contributes to 13% of the observed retreat.
- Finds that the glacier's fjord is 200 m deeper than BedMachine3 ([[Morlighem et al., 2017]]), permitting the intrusion of more warm water (0 degrees C) -> links with [[Wood et al., 2021]], highlights need to accurate bed topography ([[Morlighem et al., 2020]])
- Increased rate of undercutting explains the remaining 70% of the retreat, thus making undercutting the dominant driver of retreat.
- The agreement between the modelled and observed retreat, and the 100% explanation of the causes of retreat (split between thinning and undercutting) suggests a posteriori that the calving of grounded ice blocks does not have an impact on the retreat of Humboldt Glacier.
