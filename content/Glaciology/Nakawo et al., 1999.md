# Characteristics of Khumbu Glacier, Nepal Himalaya: recent change in the debris-covered area

## Abstract:

- **Objective**: Estimated longitudinal distribution of ablation rate and flow velocity for glacier ice covered with supraglacial debris.
- **Study Area**: Everest Base Camp (EBC) region, focusing on the ablation area.
- **Key Findings**:
    - Ablation rate:
        - Small near EBC (just below the equilibrium line).
        - Increases for about 3 km down the glacier.
        - Gradually decreases toward the terminus (located about 10 km from EBC).
    - Velocity:
        - Decreases almost linearly from EBC to the terminus.
    - Recent glacier thinning:
        - Significant thinning near EBC where the surface is mostly bare ice or thin debris cover.
        - Thinning is relatively small and uniform at lower sites, where debris cover is thicker, with supraglacial lakes and ice cliffs present.
- **Conclusion**: The general pattern aligns with field observations, and the continuity equation was used to estimate recent changes in ice thickness.

## Introduction

- It is difficult to detect the rate of melt underneath supraglacial debris through short-term observations
- It is also dangerous and difficult to carry out field observations due to the distribution of large boulders over sloping surfaces and large variations in debris thickness over small spatial scales.
	- Resulted in limited previous studies
