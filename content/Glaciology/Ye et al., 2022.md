# Monitoring glacier thinning rate in Rongbuk Catchment on the northern slope of Mt. Qomolangma from 1974 to 2021


## Abstract

- Study uses InSAR photogrammetry and laster altimetry techniques to generate 9 datasets of surface elevation differences
- Allows for geodetic mass balance
- 9 consecutive time periods between 1974 to 2021
- Main results are:
	- 1. -0.20 ± 0.03 m w.e. per year between 1974 - 2021
	- 2. Data validated by in-situ stake-observations at various altitudes and points in time
	- 3. Located a sharp surface lowering zone on the debris-covered tongue at 5240 ± 20m in the 1970s
		- Suggested that this was due to the retreat of the glacier terminus under the debris cover which moved upwards by 190 ± 20 m in the last 50 years
	- 4. Glacier contribution to run off was estimated to be 30 ± 16 % between 1974 and 2021. However, this increases to 70% between 2015-2021, demonstrating that recent warming is amplifying surface run off contributions

# Introduction

- Importance of HMA glacier changes due to importance for water resources [[Immerzeel et al., 2010]] [[Pritchard et al., 2019]]
- A sustained, accelerated glacier mass loss over HMA is reported with a doubling of the average loss during 2000–2016 by −0.43 ± 0.14 m w.e.a–1 compared to −0.22 ± 0.13 m w.e.a–1 during 1975–2000 [[Maurer et al., 2019]]
- 60% of glaciers are projected to be lost by the end of the 21st century if the melting trend continues [[Kraaijenbrink et al., 2017]] [[Rounce et al., 2020]]
- Various studies using various methods quantify MB changes in the Himalaya - but each use different methods which result in differing results and uncertainties 
- Shortage of long-term measurements also leads to uncertainties in trend estimates [[Farinotti et al., 2020]]
