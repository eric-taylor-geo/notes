#  Surface and subsurface hydrology of debris-covered Khumbu Glacier, Nepal, revealed by dye tracing

- 16 dye-tracing experiment conducted in April-May 2018 of the debris-covered Khumbu Glacier, Nepal in order to characterise its surface and subsurface drainage system.
- Long-range tracer tests showed different results to previous dye-tracing studies on other debris-covered and clean-ice glaciers – efficient, channelised systems.
- Study’s trace velocities are 1-2 orders of magnitudes lower than the inferred channelised systems.
- Results more match similar sized (but debris-free) glacier in Midtdalsbreen, Norway, which was inferred to drain through a linked-cavity system (Willis et al., 1990) -> HOWEVER, this glacier displayed much higher dispersity values.
- Khumbu Glacier displays low drainage velocities and low dispersity – implying it is neither completely channelised nor completely distributed.
- The low surface gradient may produce a low hydraulic gradient, encouraging the meandering of channels – this sinuous system could therefore result in actual transit velocities being closer to those of inferred channelised systems.
