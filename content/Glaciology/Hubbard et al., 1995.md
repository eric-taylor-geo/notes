# 

Hubbard et al.’s (1995) observations on Haut Glacier d’Arolla highlight the temporal importance of observations, in particular, the diurnal changes in subglacial hydrological activity.

Diurnal water-level variations in boreholes in the ablation area of HGA indicate the presence of a major channel which interacts with an extensive distributed hydrological system. During high pressure periods with a high meltwater flux (during the day), water is forced out of the channel into the surrounding distributed system. Low channel pressures over night (low flux) drive water back into the channel (Hubbard et al., 1995). Their findings also highlight that there is not a distinct binary between a ‘distributed’ and a ’channelised’ system – systems are likely to be both but can be predominantly one or the other dependent on conditions.
