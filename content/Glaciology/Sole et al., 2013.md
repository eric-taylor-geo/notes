# Winter motion mediates dynamic response of the Greenland Ice Sheet to warmer summers

## Abstract

- Positive correlation between melt and summer ice displacment
- However, this correlation does not extend to surface melt and **annual** ice flow - the pattern is only observed at the seasonal time scale
- Increased summer melting thereby preconditions the ice-bed interface for reduced winter motion resulting in limited dynamic sensitivity to interannual variations in surface melting.

## Conclusions

- No statistically significant correlation between normalised surface melt and annual ice flow velocity
- On seasonal timescales, ice flows faster in summer and slower in winter.
- Increased summer melting thereby preconditions the ice-bed interface for reduced winter motion resulting in limited dynamic sensitivity to interannual variations in surface melting.
- This can explain the apparently contradictory observations that show that summer velocity increases with summer temperatures and melt [[Bartholomew et al., 2011a]] [[Zwally et al., 2002]] whilst over longer time periods ice velocities increase despite increased surface melt [[Van de Wal et al., 2008]] (and also [[Tedstone et al., 2015]])
