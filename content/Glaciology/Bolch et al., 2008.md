# Identification of glacier motion and potentially dangerous glacial lakes in the Mt/ Everest region/Nepal using spaceborne imagary


## Introduction

- Chance of lake development is highest where glaciers have a low slope angle and velocity or are stagnant [[Quincey et al., 2007]]
- Whether supra/pro glacial lakes become dangerous is largely dependent on their elevation relative to the spillway over surrounding moraine [[Benn et al., 2001]] [[Sakai et al, 2007]]
- Morraine failure can be induced via earthquakes, decrease in permafrost, increase in water pressure, rock or snow slumping into lakes [[Buchroithner et al., 1982]]
- Multi-temporal remote sensing data is ideal to study the development of relief, lakes and glacier dynamics
	- It is also idea for investigating a large number of glaciers
	- Provide area-wide information of glacier activity rather than pointwise measurments
	- Understanding glacier dynamics can provide area-wide information on climate change in remote regions where weather stations may not be present [[Kääb, 2005]]
