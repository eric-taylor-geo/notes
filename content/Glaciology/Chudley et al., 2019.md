# Supraglacial lake drainage at a fast-flowing Greenlandic outlet glacier

- Store Glacier – a fast outlet glacier
- High-resolution UAV data
- Drainage transported ~ 4.8 x 106 m3of meltwater to glacier bed in 5 h, reducing lake to third of its original volume.
- No evidence of a precursor
- stages of lake drainage and GPS velocity response
- Local ice surface rose by 0.55 m, surface velocity increased from 2.0 m d−1 to 5.3 m d−1.
- Net uplift (A) and % horizontal acceleration (B) after drainage greatest ~ 4km downstream.
- Due to transient water storage constrained by bed topography, with water routed to SW (C)
- High resolution photos allow 2018 drainage mechanism to be identified.
- Advected moulin (17b) from fast & complete drainage previous year reactivated when lake edge reached it.
- Crevasse propagated back through lake allowing it to partially drain.
- Partial lake drainages may not necessarily be due to slow lake drainage as previously thought.
