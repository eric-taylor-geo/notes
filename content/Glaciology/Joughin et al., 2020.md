# A Decade of Variability on Jakobshavn Isbrae: Ocean Temperatures Pace Speed Through Influence on Mélange Rigidity

- Study presents observations of surface velocity, mélange rigidity, and surface elevation to examine the behaviour of Jakobshavn Isbræ.
- Find a pronounced behaviour of summer speed-up and thinning followed by winter slowdown and thickening.
- Extended periods of rigid mélange winters of 2016-2017 and 2017-2018, were concurrent with terminus advances ~6km farther than winters prior.
- Terminus advancement into shallower depths (Jakobshavn Isbræ has a retrograde bed) cause slowdowns and substantial thickening.
- Periods of rigid mélange coincide well with a period of cooler waters in Disko Bay.
- Ocean's dominant influence on Jakobshavn Isbræ is therefore its effect on winter mélange rigidity rather than summer submarine melting.
