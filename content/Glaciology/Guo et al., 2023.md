# Reconstruction of modified Circumpolar Deep Water intrusion and its oceanographic impact in Prydz Bay, East Antarctica

- Prydz Bay is the largest embayment in EA and is one of four primary regions for the formation of Antarctic Bottom Water [[Williams et al., 2016]]
- modified Circumpolar Deepwater reducing potential sea ice production by 45% in David Polynya [[Guo et al., 2019]] - increasing basal melt rates - Amery Ice Shelf
- Use proxy record of cadmium concentrations in Alelie penguin feathers in an ornithonogenic sediment profile to reconstruct variability in mCDW intrusion into the Prydz Bay, EA
- Freshwater input signal from diatoms and delta 18 O
- Intrusion was enhanced from 1500 to 300 yr BP - resulting in an increase in glacier discharge and a decrease in sea ice
- Weakened at 200 yr BP
- Enhanced in the last 150 years
- Intrusion into Prydz Bay appeared to be driven by the strengthening of Southern Hemisphere Westerlies -> *therefore agrees that the weakening of easterlies results in intrusion? [[Stokes et al., 2022]]*
- Based on trends, strengthening westerlies is forecasted to persist into the 21st century therefore making mCDW intrusion more continuous, threatening the stability of the ice shelf and sea ice and antarctic bottom water formation
