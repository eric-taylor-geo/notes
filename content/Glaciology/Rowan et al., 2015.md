#  Modelling the feedbacks between mass balance, ice flow and debris transport to predict the response to climate change of debris-covered glaciers in the Himalaya

- previous studies of Himalayan glaciers using models designed for clean-ice glaciers resulted in predictions of widespread, rapid deglaciation e.g., [[Shea et al., 2015]]
- However, debris-covered glaciers respond differently due to the insulating effet of debris [[Østrem, 1959]] etc. etc.
- Debris-covered glaciers lose most of their mass via surface lowering rather than terminus recession [[Hambrey et al., 2008]]
- Insulating effect of debris allows glaciers to persist at much lower elevations that what would typically be possible for a climatologically equivalent glacier [[Anderson, 2000]] [[Benn et al., 2012]]
- Role of debris will increase in the future as debris coverage increases with glacier shrinkage [[Bolch et al., 2008]] [[Kirkbride and Deline, 2013]]
- Predicting the future of the Himalayan cryosphere and water resources depends on understanding the impacts of climate change on debris-covered glaciers.

More recent studies have placed the ELA of Khumbu Glacier at 5700 m around AD2000 [[Bolch et al., 2011a]]
## Himalaya

- 14-18% of the glacier area is debris-covered [[Kääb et al., 2012]]
- increases to 36% in the Everest region (of Nepal) [[Nuimura et al., 2012]] [[Thakuri et al., 2014]]
- Between 1962 and 2011, the proportion of Everset region glaciers covered by rock debris has doubled [[Thakuri et al., 2014]]
- Limitations in the understanding of the distribution of supraglacial debris and how it evolves over time create uncertainty in the future of HMA glaciers
- Study use a novel model to model the evolution of Khumbu Glacier from the Late Holocene to 2200

## Findings:
- By 2200 the debris-covered tongue could become detached from the base of the Khumbu icefall and persist in situ, slowly reducing in size through lowering, whilst the active part of the glacier continues to recede. Supraglacial debris will then begin to accumulate on the new tongue of the active glacier near the upper part of the icefall.
