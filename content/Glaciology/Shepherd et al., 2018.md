# Mass Balance of the Antarctic Ice Sheet from 1992 to 2017


- Study combines satellite observations of the Antarctic Ice Sheet's changing volume, flow, and gravitational attraction with modelling of its surface mass balance.
- Lost 2,720 ± 1,390 billion tonnes of ice between 1992 and 2017
- Equivalent to 7.6 ± 3.9 mm of SLR
- Ocean-driven melting (pooling of CDW) has increased ice loss from WA
	- Increased from 53 ± 29 billion to 159 ±26 billion tonnes per year
- Ice shelf collapse on AP
	- Increased from 7 ± 13 billion to 33 ± 16 billion tonnes per year
- Large variations in model estimates of surface mass balance and glacial isostatic adjustment for EA - rate of mass gain over the study period is 5 ± 46 billion tonnes (YIKES)

## Main

- Antarctic ice sheets hold 58 m of SLR equivalent [[Fretwell et al., 2013]] https://doi.org/10.5194%2Ftc-7-375-2013
- Ice is channeled to the oceans through glaciers and ice streams [[Rignot et al., 2011]]
- Fluctuations in the mass of grounding ice sheets arise from the balance between snow accumulation, meltwater runoff, and ice discharge into the ocean.

- Disagreement between individual estimates of ice-sheet mass balance increases with the area of each region
- AP most agreement, EA the least
