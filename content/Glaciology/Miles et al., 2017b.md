# Spatial, seasonal and interannual variability of supraglacial ponds in the Langtang Valley of Nepal, 1999–2013

## Abstract:

- Supraglacial ponds play a key role in absorbing atmospheric energy and directing it to ice
- The spatial and temporal distribution of these features is not well documented
- Authors analyse 172 landsat scenes between 1999 and 2013
- Authors characterise the temporality of ponds, whether they are seasonal or itnerannual
- Find that pond area peaks at the monsoon area with 2% of the debris-covered area as pond
- Ponds are most frequent in zones of low surface gradient and velocity - this is also indicative of debris cover [[Scherler et al., 2011]]
- 40.5% of ponds persist over multiple years

## Data:

- 'Due to their 30 m spatial resolution, long history of repeat-visits, and free availability, the Landsat 5 and 7 satellites offered the most promise to resolve seasonal and annual patterns of supraglacial ponds' - *can apply thi reasoning to my diss*
- Landsat 5 and 7 have a return-period of 16 days - however the sensors are unable to penetrate clouds resulting in limited data availability over regions of interest.

**Cloud, shadow and snow masks**

- Landsat data analysed includes scenes that are affected by cloud cover and deep shadows - requires masking
- Authors use the Fmask algorithm [[Zhu et al., 2015]] which detects clouds spanning several spectral classes semiautomatically
