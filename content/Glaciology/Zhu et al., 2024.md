# Debris cover effects on energy and mass balance of Batura Glacier in the Karakoram over the past 20 years

## Introduction

- Stability of Karakoram glaciers over the last 30 years - "Karakoram anomally" [[Hewitt, 2005]]
- Role of debris as a possible explanation [[Hewitt, 2005]] has resulted in the mass changes of debris-covered glaciers being a focus of recent studies
- Expansion of debris over the last 30 years [[Xie et al., 2023]]
- [[Collier et al., 2015]] estimate that debris cover reduced ablation by 14% in the Karakoram
	- Attributed to thick debris cover insulation outweighing increases in melt from under thin debris

## Results and discussion 2000-2021

- Surface net radiation of the glacier accounted for the largest proportion of total energy flux (46%)
- During the accumulation period 73% of the energy flux was expended through net longwave radiation - only 15% was used for snow / ice sublimation through thermal conduction within the snow / debris layer
- In the ablation period, the energy influx was mostly from net shortwave radiation.

- The effect of supraglacial debris is strongest during the ablation season - "effectively mitigating glacier ablation"
	- "When supraglacial debris is included in energy balance processes, the snow layer absorbed a greater amount of heat from the atmosphere through thermal conduction, thereby leading to accelerated melting. As the snow progressively melted and the debris became exposed, the surface albedo experienced a rapid decline spanning from July to October. This transition resulted in the debris absorbing a greater portion of incoming shortwave radiation, much of which is returned to the atmosphere as emitted longwave radiation or sensible heat, consequently yielding a reduction in the melting energy available"
