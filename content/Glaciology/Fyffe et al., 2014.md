# A distributed energy-balance melt model of an alpine debris-covered glacier

- Development of a distributed melt model and its application to the debris covered Miage glacier, Italy over two summer seasons.
- Sub-debris melt rates are calculated using an existing debris energy-balance model.
- Melt rates for clean ice, snow and partially debris-covered ice are calculated using standard energy-balance equations.
- Simulate sub-debris melt rates compare well with ablation stake observations.
- Melt rates are highest on highest, areas of dirty crevassed ice in the middle of the glacier.
- Melt rates are lowest beneath the thickess debris on the lower glaier.
- Melt rates are highly spatially variable due to debris thickness and surface type varying significantly spatially.
- Debris delays and attenuates the melt signal compared to clean ice – peak melt occurs later in the day with increasing debris thickness.
