# Surface Melt-Induced Acceleration of Greenland Ice-Sheet Flow

## Abstract

- Coincidence of ice acceleration during the period of summer surface melting, followed by deceleration once melt ceases indicates that basal sliding is enhanced by the rapid migration of surface meltwater to the ice-bedrock interface

## Introduction

- Most ice-sheet motion is controlled by processes occurring in the lower layers - basal sliding, ice deformation, deformation of basal till
- Changes in surface climate must be transmitted deep into the ice to have a marked effect on ice flow.
- Changes in surface-mass balance alter ice thickness slowly (and therefore, the driving stress)
- Changes in temperature affect deformation rates and basal sliding - but heat is conducted slowly from the surface - signal is attenuated and lagged
- The routing of surface meltwater to the bed via moulins and crevasses provides a rapid mechanism for changes in surface dynamics (meltwater) to permeate the glacier bed.
## Results

- GPS tracking of ice finds that there is a correlation between changes in ice velocity and the intensity and timings of surface melting

**Mechanism**

- Meltwater through moulins provides widespread and continual drainage from the surface to the ice base during the melt season
- Ice base is at the pressure melting point, we base expected to be maintained.
- Water may be maintained in winter within subglacial conduits which expand during periods of increased water pressure and accelerated flow
- Increased basal water pressure is likely the cause of summer acceleration - this is a well-known mechanism for seasonal variation in alpine glacier surface velocities
