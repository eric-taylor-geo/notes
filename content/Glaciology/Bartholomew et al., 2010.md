# Seasonal evolution of subglacial drainage and acceleration in a Greenland outlet glacier

- Ice motion data obtained using GPS along a 35 km transect during the 2008 melt season
- Speed up events over the melt season migrate up-stream over the course of the summer.
- Relationship between surface melt and ice motion suggests that the seasonal evolution of the subglacial drainage system, as observed on smaller glaciers, is occurring, affecting the rate of basal slip.

## Main

- Recent studies -> [[Rignot and Kanagaratnam, 2006]] [[Joughin et al., 2008]] [[Zwally et al., 2002]] [[Van de Wal et al., 2008]]
- Hydrofracture mechanisms have been proposed which allow water to penetrate to the bed through cold ice over 1 km thick [[Van der Veen, 2007]] [[Alley et al., 2005]]
- Correlation between local surface melt and velocity fluctuations
- However, annual ablation does not necessarily lead to annual increases in ice velocites [[Van de Wal et al., 2008]] -> also [[Sole et al., 2013]]

- From the association between the onset of surface melting, surface uplift and enhanced horizontal velocities, we infer rapid delivery of surface melt water to the ice sheet bed following the establishment of a hydraulic connection
- Melt water increases basal sliding by reducing friction through hydraulic jacking or cavitation [[Anderson et al., 2015]] [[Iken et al., 1981]]
- When melt first accesses the bed, the onset of high surface velocities and uplift (phase 2_ is indicative of an inefficient basal hydraulic system in which basal water pressures are highly sensitive to relatively small inputs of water(phase 3), the gradual surface lowering and ice slow down indicates a more efficient channelized system in which basal water pressures are generally lower [[Kamb, 1987]]
