# Strong ice-ocean interaction beneath Shirase Glacier Tongue in East Antarctica

- Ice shelves with high basal melt rates are found in regions where CDW is intruding the continental shelf. CDW is warmer than water proximal to marine-terminating glaciers and ice shelves, thereby promoting melt
- WA is susceptible to ocean heat flux from CDW inflow [[Osri et al., 1995]] https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/096706379500021W
- EA's continental shelves, meanwhile, are primarily occupied by cold waters.
- However, recent hydrographic observations have shown that modified CDW intrusion is occurring beneath Totten Ice Shelf (TIS) whose continental slope is located near the eastern limb of a cyclonic gyre in the Australian-Antarctic Basin [[McCartney and Donohue, 2007]] [[Rintoul et al., 2016]]

- Shirase Glacier Tongue's continental slope is near the eastern limb of the Weddel Gyre
- SG has a small area (821 km2)
- Study finds the intrusion of warm basal water
