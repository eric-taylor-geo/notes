# A dynamic saline groundwater system mapped beneath an Antarctic ice stream

- Use magnetotelluric and passive seismic data to provide in situ measurements of a deep groundwater system within a >1 km thick sedimentary basin beneath Whillans Ice Stream.
- Deep-subglacial sediment holds an order of magnitude more water than the shallow hydrologic system above it.
- Therefore, it has the potential to modulate ice streaming through either contributing water to the subglacial hydrologic system or drawing water away
- Solutes can also result in local changes to basal melting temperatures
- Water is of marine origin, originating from a retreat in the ice sheet following deglaciation and subsequent readvance ([[Kingslake et al., 2018]])
