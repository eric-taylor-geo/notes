# Extensive retreat and re-advance of the West Antarctic Ice Sheet during the Holocene

- Boreholes drilled at multiple locations on the West Antarctic Ice Sheet allowed the recovery of subglacial till up to 200 km inland of the present Ross Sea sector grounding line.
- Boreholes contained young organic carbon, which must have originated from marine activity - microbial activity in ice uses legacy carbon trapped in ice sheets.
- Thus, the grounding line must have retreated beyond the location of these boreholes to result in marine activity.
- Evidence of a retreat of the grounding line of several hundred kilometres inland of today's grounding line, before readvancing.
- Using models, they suggest that isostatic rebounding from ice loss stabilised processes sufficiently to halt and reverse climate-initiated ice loss.
- Whether isostatic rebound can revert present-day ice losses will depend on the bedrock topography and mantle visconsity.
