# Including debris cover effects in a distributed model of glacier ablation

- Debris modelled as a probability function based upon 1000 manual thickness measurments
- Including the effect of debris in a distributed glacier melt model
- Debris reduces surface melt over Haut Glacier d'Arolla by 7% over one season
- Englacial debris melt out and rock fall contribute debris to the glacier surface, covering 10% of the glacierised area.
- Photographic records suggest debris near the tongue emerged as the glacier retreated over the last 60 years
