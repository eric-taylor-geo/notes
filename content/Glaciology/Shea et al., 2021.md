# Debris Emergence Elevations and Glacier Change

- Paper introduces a new metric - $Z_\text{DE}$ - the debris emergence elevation ,which they calculate using optical, thermal, and DE< data.
- They quantify $Z_\text{DE}$ for 975 debris-covered glaciers in HMA over 3 periods and compare $Z_\text{DE}$ with median glacier elevations, modeled ELAs, and observed rates of mass change and glacier velocity change
- Values of $Z_\text{DE}$ for individual glaciers are similar to both median glacier elevations and modeled ELAs, but slightly lower than both
- Across HMA, average value of $Z_\text{DE}$ increased at a rate of 2.7 ± 4.1 m/year between 1985 and 2017 - some increased, some decreased
- Increases in $Z_\text{DE}$ correspond with negative mass balance and decreases in glacier velocity.
- Decreases in $Z_\text{DE}$ meanwhile correspond to mass gains and increases in glacier velocity
- $Z_\text{DE}$ is a useful metric for examining regional debris-covered glacier changes and could be used to reconstruct relative mass and ELA changes in debris-covered glaciers using historic imagery and reconstructed debris-cover extents

## Methods:

- $Z_\text{DE}$ calculated from the average elevation of a polyline demarcating the boundary between debris-free and debris-covered ice.
- Debris-covered pixels are calculated using NDSI index over averaged landsat images
- *Disslink:* currently, debris cover thickness methods don't average, they just do it instantaneously in time

- Glacier mass balance changes from [[Brun et al., 2018]]
- Glacier velocity changes from [[Dehecq et al., 2019]]
- Restricted to glaciers with >10% cover below the ELA - 976 glaciers (THEN WHY DOES THE ABSTRACT SAY 975 FFS)

## Discussion

- 'While debris emergence elevations are expected to change on annual/decadal timescales in response to mass balance forcings, the timing of image acquisition could potentially affect the delineation of debris cover due to snow'
- 'This research is unable to resolve a mechanism for changes in _Z__DE_, though enhanced ablation at the boundary between ice and debris, and a positive albedo feedback'
	- Detailed energy balance modelling required for this
	- A reduction in ice flux would point towards reduced debris transport, so the correlation between reduced ice fluxes and increase in _Z__DE_ may simply be due to the previously observed relations between mass balance and velocity [[Dehecq et al., 2019]]
- Conceptual model of supraglacial debris given by [[Anderson and Anderson, 2018]] suggests that englacial debris emergence at the surface is governed by the local ablation rate and the concentration of the debris within the ice.
	- 'When debris begins to emerge and concentrate at the surface, ablation rates are reduced at debris thicknesses greater than 0.05–0.10 m, which leads to an increase in local relief of the emerging medial moraine. The increase in surface slopes promotes dispersal of the debris, and so increased mass loss promotes increased debris emergence'
- 'As the velocity is due primarily to the driving stress (with glacier thinning and mass loss leading to reduced driving stress and reduced velocities), the link between changes in velocity and _Z__DE_ may be coincidental, and not causal. Put another way, the rise in _Z__DE_ and the reduction in horizontal velocities are consequences of the same mechanism but are not directly linked. The rise in _Z__DE_ can be seen as the consequence of an increased ELA, which leaves a larger area with positive vertical velocity (downglacier of the ELA), and thus leads to debris cover expansion up-glacier. The rise in ELA leads to more thinning and thus a reduced driving stress, as highlighted in  [[Dehecq et al., 2019]].'
