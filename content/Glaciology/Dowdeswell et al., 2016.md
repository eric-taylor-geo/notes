# The variety and distribution of submarine glacial landforms and implications for ice-sheet reconstruction

-	Subglacial landforms produced by meltwater flow are an exception to the notion that subglacial landforms are streamlined.
-	Eskers and tunnel valleys tend to be sinuous, arborescent -  channel orientation likely to be in the general direction of ice flow given by the control on effective pressure by the ice-surface slope ([[Shreve, 1972]]).
-	Channels preserved in rock beds (or above e.g., eskers) may have long profiles that include both positive and negative slopes (unconstrained by the topography) – indicated water flowing under the overburden ice pressure beneath ice sheets (e.g., O Cofaigh et al., 2002)
