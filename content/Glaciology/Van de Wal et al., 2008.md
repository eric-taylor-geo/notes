# Large and Rapid Melt-Induced Velocity Changes in the Ablation Zone of the Greenland Ice Sheet

- GPS observations in the western ablation zone of the Greenland Ice Sheet
- Ice velocity reacts to increased meltwater production at the surface, increasing by a factor of for within days of injection.
- Over longer time scales, annual ice velocities decrease which suggests that the hydrological system is constantly adjusting constantly to the variable meltwater input.
- Positive-feedback appears to only be a seasonal process and has limited effect on the ice sheet response to climate warming over the next few decades - *less sensitive*

- Longer observational records are required
