# Ocean forcing drives glacier retreat in Greenland

- Study the retreat of 226 marine terminating glaciers between 1992 and 2017 using a combination of in situ, remote sensing, and modelling methods.
- 6 categories of behaviour
1. Glaciers terminating in deep water water with the detected presence of AW (74)
2. Glaciers that break into icebergs on shallow ridges, indepednent of the presence of warm atlantic water (27)
3. Glaciers that stand in shallow cold (<100 m depth) fjords with polar water (24)
4. Glaciers with long floating extensions (>10 km) (10)
5. Glaciers which already dispayed retreating behavior in 1992 (4)
6. A non-catagory for glaciers with a lack of bathymetry and ocean data (87)

- Category 1 glaciers accounted for 49% of the Greenland Ice Sheet's mass loss.
- The removal of ice tongues sometimes, but not always, resulted in glacier speed up.
- Category 2 glaciers accounted for 9% of mass loss
- Category 3 glaciers accounted for 3% of mass loss

- Ocean warming paused between 2008 and 2017; however, despite a reversion to the initial ocean state, ice fronts kept retreating, suggesting that sustained dynamic thinning at glacier margins, induced by an initial ocean warming, led to an unstable configuration of the glaciers, which could not reach a new equilibrium state.
