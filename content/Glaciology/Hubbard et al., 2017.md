#  Borehole water-level variations and the structure of the subglacial hydrological system of Haut Glacier d’Arolla, Valais, Switzerland

**Introduction:**

-	Warm based glaciers drain via a range of hydraulic configurations.
-	Existence of various drainage configurations have been analysed through proglacial bedrock mapping, dye-tracer studies, meltwater hydrochemistry, boreholes, bed observations.
-	An understanding of the co-existence and interaction between various subglacial drainage systems is critical for models glacier motion.
-	Transitions between drainage may be responsible for initiating and impeding glacial surges.
-	High and variable melt-season discharges can destabilise a system.
-	Dye-tracer studies at Haut Glacier d’Arolla show that the retreating snow line acts as the boundary between a channelised system and distributed system. (Nienow, 1994)
-	Replacement of a distributed system is laterally variable and some areas may survive the melt season due to being disconnected to channels (Fountain, 1994; Nanni et al., 2021)
-	Reduced discharge during winter results in creep closure due to increased effective pressure (due to decreased water pressure)
