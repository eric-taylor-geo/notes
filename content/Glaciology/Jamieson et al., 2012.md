# Ice-stream stability on a reverse bed slope

- Present understanding of ice-stream stability is limited by observational records that are too short to resolve muli-decadal to millennial-scale behaviour in order to validate numerical models
- Study presents a dynamic numerical simulation of Antarctic ice-stream retreat since the LGM, constrained by geophysical data - numerical ice-stream model
- Finds that the retreat of the Marguerite Bay Ice Stream was highly nonlinear - retreat was interrupted by stabilisation on a retrograde bed - whereas theory would suggest that rapid unstable retreat under these conditions [[Weertman, 1974]] [[Schoof, 2007b]]
- Transient stabilisations were caused by enhanced lateral drag as the ice stream narrowed - thus understanding ice stream width is crucial for understanding decadal- to centennial-scale ice-stream behaviour and marine ice-sheet vulnerability - time scales relevant to future forecasts for SLR over the next century
