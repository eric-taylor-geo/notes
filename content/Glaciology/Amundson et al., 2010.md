# Ice mélange dynamics and implications for terminus stability, Jakobshavn Isbræ, Greenland

- Mélange plays a role on terminus dynamics and stability.
- During winter, sea ice growth stiffens the mélange matrix by binding iceberg clasts together, ultimately preventing the calving of full-glacier-thickness icebergs, enabling terminus advance in the magnitude of several kilometres.
- The breakup of melange in summer results in terminus retreat.
- A realistic model of terminus behavior must be able to predict seasonal variations in calving rate
- We suggest that at Jakobshavn Isbræ, these variations are presently controlled by variations in sea ice cover and ice mélange strength and by dynamic thinning of grounded ice in summer
