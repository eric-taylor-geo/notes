# Rapid Holocene thinning of an East Antarctic outlet glacier driven by marine ice sheet instability

- EAIS contains more than two-thirds of the total volume of Antarctic ice grounded below sea level [[Fretwell et al., 2013]]
- Use of geological data can be used to extend the record of ice sheet observation in Antarctica
- Study reports a new terrestrial record ice ice sheet thinning from Mackay Glacier
- Suitably located because:
	- Overdeepening occurs immediately offshore of the glacier]Laterally confined - can be simulated using a one-dimensional flowline model
	- High concentration of off and onshore chronologies provide cotnent -…g., when the glacier became independent from grounded ice int he western Ross Sea
- Modelling finds that small changes in ocean temperature./ SLR were sufficient o initiate prolonged retreat into deeper water - highly sensitive
- Uses surface-exposure ages to calculate retreat
