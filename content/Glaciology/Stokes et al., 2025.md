# Warming of +1.5 °C is too high for polar ice sheets

- The thermal 'lock in' in ice melt even at current climate forcing (+1.2 degrees), as well as +1.5 degrees as per the Paris Agreement, would still generate several meters of sea-level rise over the coming centuries.
- Current trajectory is 3 degrees of warming
- Authors argue that to protect coastal areas from SLR of several meters, then the pre-industrial mean temperature needs to fall to +1 degrees.
- More work is needed to determine a safe limit for ice sheets

- Global mean sea level (GMSL) increased by around 20 cm from 1901 to 2018, with the rate of change accelerating from ~1.4 mm year−1 (1901–1990) to ~3.7 mm year−1 (2006–2018) (IPCC, 2021)
- 1 billion people within 10 m and 230 million within 1 m of sea level ([[Kulp and Strauss, 2019]])
- Important to correct for GIA, but its actual impact on melt over millennial timescales is 2% ([[Aschwanden et al., 2019]]) -> albeit, spatially variable (See [[Larour et al., 2019]])
