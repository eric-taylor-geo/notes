# Direct Observation of the Mechanism of Glacier Sliding Over Bedrock

Observations by Kamb and LaChapelle, 1964 at Blue Glacier, Washington, confirmed that both regelation and enhanced creep were taking place at the base of the glacier using direct observations from a tunnel close to the side of the glacier.

The decreasing effectiveness of regelation flow and increasing effectiveness of enhanced creep with increased obstacle size, implies that there is an obstacle size where ice flow is least efficient. Kamb and LaChapelle (1964) found that the combined effects of regelation and enhanced are least efficient at bumps of around 50 cm in length.
