# Terrestrial glint seen from deep space: Orientated ice crystals detected from the Lagrangian point

- polychromatic EPIC imaging camera sees unexpected bright flashes of light over both ocean and land.
- Authors construct a yearlong time series of flash latitudes, scattering angles, and oxygen absorption to demonstrate that the flashes over land are specular reflections of tiny ice platelets floating in the area nearly horizontally - tf that is so cool
- Such detection of tropospheric ice can be used to constrain the likelihood of orientated crystals and their contribution to the Earth's planetary albedo
