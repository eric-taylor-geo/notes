# Climate Projections Very Likely Underestimate Future Volcanic Forcing and Its Climatic Effects

Even if smaller eruptions' individual impact on the climate is relatively low, their combination can result in cooling, which is likely underestimated in current models of future volcanic forcing on the climate.
"These small-magnitude eruptions may not have a measurable effect individually, but collectively, their effect is significant."
Small-magnitude eruptions are responsible for as much as 50% of all sulphur gases emitted by volcanoes
