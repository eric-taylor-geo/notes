- Consider a parcel of fluid that conserves absolute vorticity in a westerly current:
$$
f + \zeta = \text{const}
$$
- The parcel is perturbed northward. Therefore, the planetary vorticity increases. 
- In order to maintain absolute vorticity, the relative vorticity must decrease. 
- This induces clockwise movement which bends the air parcel back southward.
- Now, planetary vorticity decreases, and relative vorticity increases in order to conserve absolute vorticity
- Restoring force is due to the variation in how the Earth's rotation is felt at different latitudes.

**Rossby Wave Propagation Mechanism**

**Analytical approach:**

The dispersion relation for Rossby waves is:

$$
\omega = Ul - \frac{\beta l}{l^2 + m^2}
$$
Where $U$ is the mean flow, $l$ and $m$ are the zonal and meridional wave numbers, and $β$ is the beta approximation ($\beta =  \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}$)
The phase speed is: 
$$
c = \frac{\omega}{l}
$$
Therefore, the phase speed for Rossby waves is:
$$
c_{\text{Rossby}} = U - \frac{\beta}{l^2+m^2}
$$
Interpretation
- $\frac{\beta}{l^2+m^2}$ is always positive. Therefore, relative to the mean flow, Rossby waves propagate in the negative-x direction (Westward)
- Longer waves travel faster
- Waves close to the equator travel faster 

**Physical understanding**

- Can be understood in terms of the conservation of absolute vorticity. When a parcel of fluid changes latitude, to compensate for its changing planetary vorticity, it must acquire either positive or negative relative vorticity. This induces a circulation that leads to westward propagation of the disturbance
