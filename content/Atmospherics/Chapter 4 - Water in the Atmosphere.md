[[Atmospherics - Clouds]]
## Condensation and Deposition: Cloud Formation

**Solute and Curvature Effects**

- As a volume of unsaturated air cools, its RH increases. Upon sufficient cooling, the air reaches the dew point, and the RH reaches 100%.
- Condensation should occur at this point.
- However, cloud droplets can form at RHs other than 100%.
- The **solute effect** allows for the growth of droplets below RH of 100%. This is because salt particles dissolved in water droplets attract water molecules more than the neighbouring water molecules. Therefore, the rate of evaporation is reduced.
- By suppressing evaporation, the solute effect enhances droplet growth by condensation, allowing cloud formation at RH below 100%. However, as the droplet grows, the solution becomes more dilute, reducing the solute effect - Rault's Law
- Cloud droplets are curved - on a curved surface water droplets have fewer neighbours attracting eachother than on a flat surface. Therefore, evaporation can occur more easily - the **curvature effect** or **kelvin effect**
- This effect opposes the formation of small droplets by condensation - resultantly, the RH must be above 100% (supersaturated) for cloud formation to occur.
- For water droplets to occur by themselves, RHs of over 200% are required, which does not occur naturally in the atmosphere

**Nucleation**

- **Nucleation** is the initial formation of a cloud droplet around any type of particle.
- **Homogenous nucleation**: the droplet is formed only by water molecules
	- Requires water molecules to have very low kinetic energy, else clusters of water molecules cannot form. Therefore, this only occurs below -40 celcius
- **Heterogeneous nucleation:** non-water molecules act as condensation nuclei for cloud droplet formation.

**Condensation Nuclei**

- **Hygroscopic**: 'water-seeking' nuclei - can allow for droplet formation below 100% RH
- **Hydrophobic**
- Sources of aerosols: dust, sea salt spray, pollen, spores, sulphur
- Ice crystal formation is more complex than water droplet formation - molecules ust fit into the shape of the crystal rather than haphazardly depositing on the surface.
- Ice nuclei make deposition easier.
- Ice particles can form through deposition, freezing, immersion and contact nucleation.
- **Deposition nucleation**: Ice forms from vapour by deposition onto the ice nucleus when the air is supersaturated with respect to ice. Happens most often on particles that have a molecular geometry resembling that of ice, which aids water molecules to align in the proper molecular structure for forming ice when they are deposited.
- **Freezing nucleation** occurs when a supercooled droplet freezes without the aid of a non-water particle. Small water droplets are able to become supercooled due to the process by which freezing occurs. When below 0 degrees, for ice to form, all the water molecules must align themselves in the proper crystalline structure. An initial alignment of molecules then allows the rest to follow quickly. Therefore, in smaller water droplets, the probability of molecules aligning is reduced - allowing droplets to become supercooled.
- **Immersion nucleation**: the nucleus is submerged in a liquid drop and upon reaching a given temperature the immersed nucleus allows the supercooled liquid to rapidly freeze
- **Contact nucleation**: ice nuclei collides with a supercooled droplet, resulting in immediate freezing

## Clouds and the GH Effect

- Clouds reflect shortwave radiation and absorb and re-emit longwave radiation from the Earth's surface.
- The balance between the two dictates a cloud's contribution to the GH effect.
- Cold clouds, e.g., cirrus clouds, have a cold temperature, and therefore despite their absorption of longwave radiation emitted by the Earth's surface, according to the Steffan Boltzman law, they emit very little energy due to their cold temperature.
- Meanwhile, lower clouds which have temperatures more similar to the Earth's surface have a closer radiation loss to space than the Earth's surface.
- A cloud's effectiveness at reflecting sunlight is related to how large its water droplets / ice crystals are. Smaller particles result in a higher albedo and more reflection.
- Therefore, the influence of clouds on the Earth's energy balance is a function of the clouds' spatial extend, their thickness, their altitude, and how large their particles are.
