# The possibilities of polar meteorology, environmental remote sensing, communications and space weather applications from Artificial Lagrange Orbit

## Introduction

- Potential for satellites to be stationed in Lagrange orbits (LO) has been known for some time [[Lagrange, 1892]] [[Farquhar, 1968]]
- Recent research has suggested that Artificial Lagrange Orbits (ALO) are possible through the usage of solar sails [[McInnes, 1999]] [[McInnes and Mulligan, 2003]]
- The use of ALO could permit views of the Earth's polar regions, where continuous monitoring is unavailable from geostationary satellites 
- Polar imagary is rather made up of mosaics from merging different instruments on different satellites to provide a high spatial and temporal coverage for the polar regions [[Lazzara, 2004]]
