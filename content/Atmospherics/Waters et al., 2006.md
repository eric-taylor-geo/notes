# The Earth observing system microwave limb sounder (EOS MLS) on the aura Satellite

- EOS MLS measures several chemical species, cloud ice, temperature, and geopotential height to improve the understanding of stratospheric ozone chemistry, the interaction of composition and climate, and pollution in the upper troposphere
- Measures are made simultaneously and continuously during day and night
- Uses heterodyne radiometers that observe thermal emission from the atmospheric limb in broad spectral regions:
	- 118, 190, 240, 650 GHz and 2.5 THz
- Launched July 15 2014 
- Atmospheric limb scan is performed routinely every 25 seconds - resulting in a vertical profile retrieved every 165 km along the suborbital track
