# Evolution of satellite observations in the United States and their use in meteorology

Read in [[Barry and Chorley, 2010]] textbook

- Launching of meteorological satellites revolutionised meteorology
- First pictures were transmitted on 1 April 1960
- Satellite pictures captured mesoscale weather systems were which too large to be seen by ground observers and too small to be detected by networks of synoptic systems
- Automatic Picture Transmission (APT) to ground stations begin in 1963
- Operational polar-orbiting satellites were followed in 1966 by geostationary satellites 
- Early photographic systems were replaced in the mid-1960s by radiometric sensors in the visible and infrared wavelengths - initially moderate spatial resolution but since improved
- First atmospheric temperature profiles from [[Nimbus]] satellites.
- [[Schmetz and Menzel, 2015]]
