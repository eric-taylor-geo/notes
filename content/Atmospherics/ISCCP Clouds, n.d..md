# International Satellite Cloud Climatology Project - Clouds

- Clouds' most important roles in climate are to modulate the Earth's basic radiation balance and produce precipitation.
- Clouds reflect incoming solar radiation and inhibit the radiation of heat radiation from the Earth's surface - thereby affecting both sides of the energy balance equation (in = out)
- Precipitation produced from water vapour releases latent heat to the atmosphere (the evapration of water vapour from the surface cools it, therefore these processes transfer heat form the surface to the atmosphere)
- Therefore, changes in clouds will modify the radiative energy balance and water exchanges that determine the climate.
- Clouds themselves are produced by the climate - therefore this is a feedback loop.
- The complex relationships between climate and clouds makes it difficult to determine their net effect on the energy and water balances in the atmosphere.
