## 3.1 Accumulation and Coarse Modes

- Atmospheric aerosols are polydisperse - they exhibit a range of sizes
- Aerosol populations are therefore characterised by their size distribution
- Sizes span several orders of magnitude - from a few nanometers formed from nucleation to hundreds of micrometres produced by wind friction on the land and ocean surface
- Different modes are defined: (small to large)
	- Nucleation (or ultrafine) mode <0.01
	- Aitken Mode (sometimes referred to with the nucleation mode as the fine mode) 0.01 - 0.1
	- Accumulation mode  0.1 - 1
	- Coarse mode - radius larger than 1 micrometer
		- e.g, desert dust and sea salt
	- Supercoarse mode
- Usual to see three+ modes within a population's size distribution

## 3.3 Chemical composition

- The chemical composition controls the:
	- Hygroscopicity (ability to take up water and grow in size as ambient relative humidity increases)
	- The ease at which it can be scavenged by rain
	- Ability to act as CCN or IN
	- Optical properties

**Aerosol Mixture**

- External mixture: particles are chemically pure, and the mixture is comprised of particles of different chemical compositions - therefore, it is possible to differentiate the particles according to their distinct chemical composition.
	- A different size distribution for each aerosol type
- Perfect Internal mixture: all particles have the same chemical composition and the aerosol population can be characterised by a single size distribution
- Reality lies within these two extremes
- Aerosol mixing occurs through the coagulation of particles of different chemical composition and through the condensation of semi-volatile compounds on pre-existing particles

## 3. 5 Deliquescence, Efflorescence, and Hystersis

- Deliquescence point - where an aerosol transitions from the solid to the liquid phase - this can result in the sudden expansion of an aerosol particle
- Size variations experienced by such an aerosol particle are different for increasing and decreasing relative humidities, resulting in a hysteresis effect.
- Efflorescence: critical relative humidity for an aerosol particle presently in a metastable state to crystalise
