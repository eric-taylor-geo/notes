# Preconditioning the 2023 Kahramanmaraş (Türkiye) earthquake disaster


East Anatolian fault has been quiet since 1971, accumulating plate-motion strain at 10 mm yr-1 over the pat 500 years, thereby storing ~5 meters of potential slip.
Co-seismic displacement of 4-6 meters released during the main shock agrees well with the expected amount

## *Exposure*

- Spatial distribution of people and the condition of the buildings in which they reside
- 13 million people experiencd moderate to high levels of ground shaking in a region where health services were already dealing with a high number of covid-19 related illnesses
- 2.9 million internally displacd people in northern Syria were affected
- The initial mainshock struck at 04:17 am local time, when most people were inside their homes and therefore exposed to building damage.
- The ting also coincided with a winter storm, meaning the people who managed to escape their homes were subequently expsed to freezing temperature of -5 to -19 degrees C outside. Heaby snow also blocked roads and railways, which complicated search and resuce, and prevented aid from reaching affected areas on the same day.

## Buildings and regulations

- Images of completely collapsed buildings and the high death toll are a stark reminder that poor building practices are a major contributor to turning earthquakes into major disasters
- Reinforced concrete buildings in Türkiye are supposed to conform to detailed standards, which were last updated in 2018

## Poverty

- The poor generally do not own any property and are more likely to live in houses that are old and/or illegally built.
- 6.4 million refugees and displaced people in Türkiye and northern Syria were exposed to earthquake shaking. Refugees and impoverished locals are often more vulnerable to natural hazards as they are likely to live in overcrowded conditions and/or poorer quality housing.
