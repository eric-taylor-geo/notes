# Floods and the COVID-19 pandemic—A new double hazard problem

- Flooding is a serious problem that could complicate the significant and wide-ranging measures countries have taken to fight the coronavirus pandemic
- community sandbagging is more difficult when everyone is practicing social distancing and people are in self-isolation. During emergency evacuation, practicing social distancing can be very difficult, even impossible.
- The economic damage being caused by the virus has also destabilized people's jobs and financial welfare, limiting their ability to absorb unexpected costs from flooding
- People will break quarantine to respond to rising waters.
- Thus, the actions to mitigate one hazard completely nullify the actions to mitigate another occurring simultaneously and independently.
- The coincidence of floods and COVID-19 is a real issue of the multihazard type, where the underlying hazards refer to independent events
