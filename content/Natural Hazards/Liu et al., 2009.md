# Slow earthquakes triggered by typhoons

## Main

- Taiwan experiences typhoons frequently - mainly during the second half of the year
- Lowering of air pressure during a typhoon results in a corresponding episode of expansion (positive strain) in the strain meter data - amplitude of the excursion is proportional to the pressure decrease and is also dependent on the bulk modulus of the surrounding rock
- However, negative strain changes (contraction) have also been observed to coincide with typhoons - authors identify 20 contraction events between 2002 and 2007
