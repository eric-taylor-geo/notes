# Detailed tephrochronology and composition of major Holocene eruptions from Avachinsky, Kozelsky, and Koryaksky volcanoes in Kamchatka

- Even small eruptions from this volcanic group and associated ash falls and lahars pose high risks for the population and the existing infrastructure
- The volcanic group includes (from E to W) the dormant Kozelsky, active Avachinsky, and Koryaksky volcanoes and a number of eruptive monogenetic vents as well as the extinct Aag and Arik volcanoes. The volcanic centres form a prominent chain extending from the volcanic front into the rear-arc perpendicular to the deep-sea trench
