# Huge volcanic eruptions: time to prepare

- The world is woefully unprepared for such an event. The Tongan eruption should be a wake-up call. Recent data from ice cores suggest that the probability of an eruption with a magnitude of 7 (10 or 100 times larger than Tonga) or greater this century is 1 in 6 [[Lin et al., 2022]][^1]
- Eruptions of this size have, in the past, caused abrupt climate change and the collapse of civilisations, and have been associated with the rise of pandemics [[Newhall et al., 2018]][^2]
- Yet, little investment has gone into limiting what an eruption of this magnitude could do. Impacts would cascade across transport, food, water, trade, energy, finance, and communication in our globally connected world.
- Large-scale volcanic eruptions will have global impacts. Yet, despite this, the global response to mitigating their impacts is considerably lower than other global-affecting hazards which are significantly less likely.
- For instance, 'Planetary defence' receives hundreds of millions of dollars in funding each year, and has several global agencies devoted to it.
	- NASA's Double Asteroid Redirect Test (DART) mission in 2022 cost $324.5 million [^3]
- By contrast, there is no coordinated action, nor large-scale investment, to mitigate the global effects of large-magnitude eruptions. This needs to change.

## Recurrence of large eruptions

- Analysing sulfate spikes in the stratigraphic record can determine the recurrence rate.
- Between 60 and 9 ka, VEI 7 eruptions occur every 625 years [[Lin et al., 2022]]
- VEI 8 eruptions (super-eruptions) occur every 14,300 years
- These suggestions are more frequent than previous assessments which round recurrence intervals of 1,200 and 17,000 years, respectively [[Rougier et al., 2018]][^4]
