# Seismic Risk Cartographic Visualisation for Crisis Management

- Use GIS maps of risks and vulnerabilities for the territory under study 
	- Obtain distribution of earthquake intensities and peak ground motion accerations
	- Determine fragility laws and vulnerability functions for buildings and different structure types
	- Determine vulnerability functions and laws of earthquake impact on population
	- Estimate damage due to scenario events according to maps of seismic zoning or possible earthquake source zones maps
	- Estimate dage due to co-lateral hazards
	- Compute individual and collective seismic risk and risks due to other hazards
- Reliability of loss and risk assessment is dependent on:
	- Completeness and reliability of databases on elements at risk (population and built environment) and hazard sources
	- Reliability of vulnerability functions and fragility laws of elements at risk
	- Relevance and reliability of seismic hazard maps with different details
- All simulation models and data bases bring in their own uncertainties and propagate the uncertainties in previous steps of estimation procedure


- Maps of seismic risk have been constructed for P-K
- Building inventory for cities is verified and the percents of different building types and average heights are obtained through inspection.
