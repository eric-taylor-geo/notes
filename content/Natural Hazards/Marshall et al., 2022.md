# Volcanic effects on climate: recent advances and future avenues

Notes mostly relevant for first supo essay

- volcanic eruptions have been the most important internal mechanism for natural causes of climate change for millennia [[Hergerl et al., 2013]] [[Schurer et al., 2013]]
- Understanding their impact on climate is vital for investigating how climate responds to external forcing and for predicting the future volcanic impacts that may influence society
- Fundamentals on volcanic eruptions on the climate are well understood 
- Cooling and heating cascade into a variety of further impacts:
	- Cooling can reduce precipitation and ocean heat content
	- Heating can change circulation in the atmosphere
	- Aerosols themselves can impact atmospheric chemistry leading to ozone depletion
	- Review from [[Robock, 2000]]
- Intricacies of wider impacts are less understood than the overall impact on radiation and surface temperature - owing to a lack of observations from large-magnitude eruptions - much of our knowledge is derived from 1991 Pinatubo eruption
- Discrepancies between simulated and observed or reconstructed responses indicate that our understanding of the known-on effects is incomplete

**Holuhuran, Iceland**

- 2014-2015 eruption which emitted gas primarily into the troposphere
- Resulted in increased reflectivity of clouds through aerosol interaction causing a radiative forcing of climate [[Gettelman et al., 2015]] [[Malavele et al., 2017]] [[McCoy and Hartmann, 2015]] [[Schmidt et al., 2010]]

- Improvements in the techniques used for measuring sulfur isotopes from ice cores have allowed better indication of whether sulfur emissions were injected into the stratosphere or merely remained in the troposphere [[Burke et al., 2019]]
- 
Despite the absence of large-magnitude eruptions in the last few decades, advances have been made on the impact of volcanic eruptions ont the climate through more comprehensive datasets.
