# Critical Airpot Infrastructure Disaster Resilience: A Framework and Simulation Model for Rapid Adaptation

## Introduction

- Failures in critical infrastructures can leave populations without essential supplies such as food, water, or healthcare ([[Hallegatte et al., 2019]])
- The revival of critical hub infrastructures is vital for the recovery of an affected region [[Hallegatte et al., 2019]] [[Comes and de Walle, 2014]] -> *obvious coursework link*
	- Seaports / airports may receive incoming shipments for reconstructing damaged areas
- Thus, critical hub infrastructure must not only resume its pre-disaster level of performance but also exceed it to cope with an increased operational load
- A disaster has two immediate effects on an airport:
	- Firstly, the partial disruption to the airport and reduction in its capacity
	- Secondly, the airport finds itself in a new role as a disaster relief logistics hub
