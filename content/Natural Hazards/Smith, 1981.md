# The _b_-value as an earthquake precursor

- Study of Earthquakes in NZ
- Computes a rolling b-value in time using the last 50 events
- Finds that the b-value increases in the 100s of days before an earthquake and then decreases in the 10s of days prior.
- Therefore suggests that the initial increase then quick decrease can act as a precursor of an earthquake.
- A decreasing b-value signifies that relative to smaller earthquakes, larger earthquakes are more common, as dictated by the Guttenberg-Richter law [[Gutenberg and Richter, 1944]]
