# A participatory approach to determine the use of road cut slope design guidelines in Nepal to lessen landslides

- Road cut slope failures are a type of landslide process and are extensive across the road network of Nepal.
- Over 10 % of global rainfall-triggered fatal landslides occur in Nepal, despite it having less than 0.4 % of the global population [[Froude and Petley, 2018]]
- Complex interplay between natural and anthropogenic processes in Nepal [[KC et al., 2024]]
- Most significant natural triggers of slope instability in Nepal are tectonic activity (through rock movement and earthquakes) and rainfall (resulting in mass erosion and the reduced strength of rocks and soils) [[Hearn and Shakya, 2017]]
- Most documented anthropogenic activity contributing to slope instability in Nepal is the rapid and haphazard construction of roads [[Hearn and Shakya, 2017]]
- 70% of slope failures on mountain roads are cut slope failures rather than larger 'natural' landslides [[Hearn, 2011]]
	- Cut slopes are slopes that are excavated adjacent to a road which is often steeper than the surrounding topography - mountainsides are cut into to widen roads
- Extensive cut slope failures are partly blamed on the limited current guidelines used by engineers to design the geometry of cut slopes [[Paudyal et al., 2023]]
- Shallower cut slopes require more excavation and are therefore more costly
- Determining a safe slope angle is dependent on the slope material.
- Current guidelines do not sufficiently consider strength of material
