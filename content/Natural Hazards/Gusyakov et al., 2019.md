# In Russian:  Regional Tsunami Hazard Maps for the Far East Coast of the Russian Federation Built in the Framework of the PTHA Methodology

![[Gusyakov translated.pdf]]
1. The task of assessing long-term tsunami hazard is largely analogous to the task of assessing seismic hazard. The latter is currently addressed in all countries based on the PSHA methodology, which is also the basis for the project to create a general seismic zoning map of the territory of Russia OSR-2016. In carrying out this project, the tsunami hazard from underwater earthquakes was unfortunately not taken into account, although it is the main concern for the Far Eastern region, where about 90% of all strong earthquakes in Russia occur. However, the results of the extensive work carried out within the framework of the OSR-2016 project to refine catalogs of historical earthquakes, study the structure of their foci, and map earthquake occurrence zones in tsunami-prone areas can and should be used to assess tsunami hazard for the Far Eastern and other coasts of Russia.
2. The PTHA methodology represents an effective and sufficiently justified approach to solving the problem of probabilistic assessment of tsunami hazard for the coast. Based on it, both overview tsunami zoning of the coasts of major regions and detailed tsunami hazard maps for individual settlements, harbors, and ports are produced. Despite all the existing shortcomings of this methodology, both fundamental and technical (for criticism of probabilistic approaches to assessing seismic hazard, see, for example, ), it is currently an adequate engineering tool for creating tsunami hazard maps of various scales and, after necessary adaptation, can and should be used in mapping tsunami hazard on the coasts of Russia.
3. The version of the PTHA methodology presented in the article is implemented as a web application WTMap, which has access to the entire volume of observational information related to the tasks of tsunami zoning of the coast and calculation program packages. The application allows for interactive assessments of expected tsunami heights and their recurrence and to map them in specific areas of the Far Eastern coast of the Russian Federation. The obtained estimates can be quickly recalculated when replacing observational catalogs with more complete and reliable ones, when adding previously missing events or revising their parameters, as well as the results of new scenario calculations.
4. The main problems in applying the developed methodology of probabilistic tsunami zoning to specific coastal areas are related to the construction of seismotectonic models of tsunami-generating zones threatening this coast, based on incomplete and not always reliable information about the geological structure of these zones and their seismotectonic regime. The main problem here is the insufficient length of instrumental and historical catalogs, the duration of which often does not exceed one main seismic cycle in subduction zones (140 ± 50 years). Certain difficulties also arise in obtaining digital models of the seabed and land reliefs capable of providing the necessary accuracy of results. One way to overcome the difficulties associated with the insufficiency of instrumental and historical data is to use the results of searching for and studying traces of paleotsunamis. Such work on the Far Eastern coast of the Russian Federation has been carried out for almost thirty years and should also be performed on other marine coasts of Russia.
5. A separate problem is the reduction of computational labor intensity of large series of scenario calculations necessary to obtain estimates of tsunami hazard for large return periods (1000 years and more). This is possible through the development of mathematical technology that reduces the number of necessary scenario calculations, in particular by accelerating these calculations through the use of new mathematical models and algorithms, modern hardware and software tools.
