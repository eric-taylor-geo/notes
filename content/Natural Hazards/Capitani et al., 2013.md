# The slope aspect: a predisposing factor for landsliding?

- Southern-facing slopes are most favourble to landsliding in semi-arid environments - southern slopes dry more rapidly during sunny conditions which leads to larger expansion / contraction cycles in the slope regolith which produces superfiial cracks.
- During rainfall events, rainwater percolates downward quickly in the superficial cracks as the slope load increases and the sheer strength of the slope material decreases.
