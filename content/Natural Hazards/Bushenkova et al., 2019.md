# Tomographic Images of Magma Chambers Beneath the Avacha and Koryaksky Volcanoes in Kamchatka


- Avacha and Koryaksky are 'home volcanoes' for P-K - 25-30km away
- Represent a serious potential hazard for the city
- P-K was built on pyroclastic flows ejected by a catastrophic explosion which occured c. 30,000 years ago [[Melekestev et al., 1992]]
- Avacha is well studied due to its proximity
![[Pasted image 20250322154413.png]]
- Avacha is one of the most active volcanoes in Kamchatka
- In this study, for the first time, we have robustly mapped the 3‐D structure of two upper‐crustal magma reservoirs beneath the active Avacha and Koryaksky volcanoes. This information is extremely important  for creating realistic models of the plumbing systems of these volcanoes, in order to provide more reliable forecasts of their activity
