# Estimation and Abatement of the Urban Seismic Risk

- Building vulnerability is an increasingly decisive and controllable factor of seismic safety -> therefore we can and must manage seismic risk.
- Programme for Risk Analysis (PRANA) has been implemented in the seismic-prone urbanised region (SPUR) of Kamchatka 
- Increase in public awareness of seismic risk
- DISC: disaster scenarios
## Introduction and Background

- the seismic safety of old settlements and big cities is becoming more pressing than the seismic stability of new buildings which are designed in accordance with modern seismic codes
- Increases in population density and industrial concentration causes numerous secondary disasters -> which can mean less intensive earthquakes can have devastating impacts on a vulnerable and unprepared seismic region.
- The resistance of buildings to earthquakes determines the urban vulnerability to earthquakes, the potential direct damage and hence, as per the disaster risk equation, the seismic risk.
- Two main groups within the built environment:
	- Residential buildings and common industrial facilities 
	- Special structures, lifelines, and facilities

- When it comes to earthquake disasters, it is unrealistic to prevent the disaster from happening in the first instance. Therefore, reasonable disaster mitigation policy is required, where the disaster is not seen as being socially unacceptable, but rather, it is seen as permissible.

## Programme for Risk Management (PRIMA)

- Strengthening buildings
- Reinforcing bridges
- Programme of preventative seismic strengthening of structures (PRESESS)

- DISC in P-K considers:
	- Night-time, winter, seismic impacts of 8-9 MSK-intensity (worse case)
	- Secondary effects of tsunami, snow avalanches, soil in-stability, explosions and fires, hazardous chemical storages and facilities (ammonia and chlorine)
	- Electric and heat power and water supply facilities and lifelines
