# Fingerprinting subduction margins using principal component analysis profiles: A data science approach to assessing earthquake hazard

- Difficult to assess the recurrence of giant earthquakes (Mw ≥ 8.5) along subduction margins due to their long recurrence times - not all margins have experienced giant earthquakes since 1900, the start of the well-documented seismic record (McCarrrey 2008)
- Thus this raises the question of whether certain geological margin properties enable or inhibit large earthquakes (e.g., Ruff, 1989). Or, alternatively, all subduction margins can initiate large earthquakes and the hitherto lack of them is due to the short earthquake record (McCaffrey, 2008)

- Study uses unsupervised learning to explore correlations between sediment thickness, relative plate velocity, subducting plate's dip angle and roughness, and maximum magnitude.
- Results suggest that connections between margin properties and seismic behaviour are non-linear
