# At-Scale Model Output Statistics in mountain environments (AtsMOS v1.0)

Summary:

- Uses machine learning methods to downscale weather predictions from coarse numerical meteorological predictions to local points.
- Applications for summit of Mt. Everest - model improves the prediction of wind speed and temperature and other derivative variables crucial for mountaineering safety. 

## Introductory points

- Major topographic variations in mountain environments cause large spatial variability in the weather - reality can diverge substantially from numerical weather prediction grid-point forecasts (Zhang et al., 2022)
- Consistent biases can be corrected for (e.g., elevation through knowledge of the lapse rate; Minder et al., 2010) - the impact of unresolved processes e.g., local valley or glacier winds (Khadka et al., 2022) is challenging to correct.

## Mount Everest weather data

- 3 vertical pressure levels (350, 400 and 450 hPa)
- Use geopotential height from the three pressure levels to linearly interpolate or extrapolate to a fourth vertical level
