# Pleistocene-Holocene Monogenetic Volcanism at the Malko-Petropavlosvsk Zone of Transverse Discolations on Kamchatka: Geochemical Features and Genesis

- Monogenetic volcanism produces small-volume volcanoes (volume of edifice ≤ 1 km3), with a wide range of eruptive styles, such as cinder (scoria) cones, extrusive domes, maars and tuff rings (Nemeth & Kereszturi, 2015). 
- Monogenetic volcanoes are also defined by short eruptive periods and dispersed plumbing systems, although the magmatic systems to which they belong may be long-lived (Smith & Nemeth, 2017)
- Future volcanic eruptions within a monogenetic volcanic field are not easy to forecast due to appearing in unexpected locations and sometimes using unknown faults or are not apparently associated with any known surface expression of structural elements
- Geophysical and geochemical data suggests that seismic activity may start 1-2 years before the eruption of a monogenetic volcano however activity only intensifies to waring levels 2-3 months or even as little as 1-2 weeks before an eruption [[Albert et al., 2016]]
- Multiple magma batches can interact between themselves in a subvolcanic reservoir and magma plumbing system, and numerous intrusions can occur on similar time scales, as recorded by increased seismicity and gas emanation according to the seismic, petrological and geochemical monitoring [[Albert et al., 2016]]
- Resultantly, the study of monogenetic volcanism is difficult
- However, if monogenetic volcanism is associated with stratovolcanoes which evolve over stable and long-lived magna sources and conduits, their volcanic hazards can be combined
- Ascending magma in monogenetic eruptions may rupture suddenly which is a major hazard in urban areas

## Volcanism in the MPZ

- Volcanism in the MPZ has two forms
	- Parasitic cones on the slopes of associated polygenetic stratovolanoes
	- Independent monogenetic cinder cones and extrusive domes
- Best example of an extrusive dome is Mount Mishennaya, located in downdown P-K, with a height of 383 m and a base diameter of ~1x2 km
	- Dated to roughly 600 ± 200 ka
- All other extrusive domes are covered by the debris avalanches of the Avachinsky volcano which occured 30 ka BP [[Ponomareva et al., 2006]]
- Other domes exist e.g., underwater based on bathymetry and geophysical investigations [[Dmitriev and Ezhob, 1977]]
- Study suggests effort into gathering data wrt to the age, mode of emplacement and composition as well as relation (or lack of) to other stratovolcanoes in the area in order to understand the area's volcanism better
- Lack of a geochronology for monogenetic cones
- Avachinsky volcano had two stages of volcanic activity in the Holocene:
	- 1st: 3.8 -8 ka ka bp -  rare by significant pyroclastic eruptions
	- 2nd: 3.8 ka to present -  more frequent pyroclastic explosions and lavas of basaltic andesitic magma compositions [[Krasheninnikov et al., 2020]]
- Magmatic activity has shifted to the plumbing system of Avachinksy volcano, coincident with an activation of monogenetic volcanism

## Importance of Studying Monogenetic Volcanism for Society

- MPZ holds the main gateway to Kamchatka. Its main cities are located within MPZ faults and could potentially be affected by eruptions of monogenetic volcanoes, as well as seismic activity along these faults
- 27 monogenetic volcanoes have been identified so far in this report
- Installment of more seismic stations will help to distinguish tectonic earthquakes along the fault and volcanically triggered earthquakes
- Ground-surface monitoring using InSAR
- Monogenetic volcanism formed during the Holocene (Supplementary file 1) also provides positive aspects to the population by creating recreational and touristic opportunities of vistas and isolated islands; these are currently utilized as a basement for lighthouses in the Avacha Gulf and for cell and radio/TV transmission stations (such as those on Mt. Mishennaya in downtown Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky;
- Many life-line infrastructures are associated with these volcanic features. Cinder cones on the coastline are quarried for road and construction material, recently used, for example, at fish processing plants. The Mount Mishennaya extrusive dome was quarried before 1990, and one monogenetic cinder cone near Vilyuchinsk is a large factory specializing in the development of gravels and sands
- The elevated grounds of local coastal cinder cones could provide good vertical escape shelter and routes for potential tsunamis.
- As most of the population of Kamchatka lives close to these monogenetic cinder cones and extrusive domes, it is necessary to establish a continuous monitoring system using seismic data, ground deformation, and gas and water analysis to predict potential dangers of volcanic hazard and geothermal benefits from monogenetic volcanoes in the future.
