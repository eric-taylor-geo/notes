# Predicting Evacuation in Two Major Disasters: Risk Perception, Social Influence, and Access to Resources

- People deciding not to evacuate is a combination of individual characteristics and 3 social psychological processes:
1. Risk Perception
2. Social Influence
3. Access to resoruces
- A multifaceted and tailored approach to both individuals and communities is needed - simple warning is not enough

## Introduction

- Evacuation: "the mass physical movement of people in a community, is temporary in nature and emerges in order to cope with community threats, damages, or disruptions (Quarentelli, 1985)" (p. 918)
- Evacuation requires involvement from the community and the individual. Yet, ultimately the individual is responsible for their decision to evacuate.
- Understanding who is at a higher risk of not evacuating (and why) is crucial for implementing strategies to provide the necessary resources for successful evacuation.

## Risk Perceptions and Protection Motivation

- Decision-making research - accessing important factors to citizens who have been told to evacuate and how they decide whether to comply with the issued warning (Perry and Lindell, 1991)
- Decisions are made, taking into account "perceived susceptibility to the threat, perceived efficacy, preventative action, and the barriers and costs" (p. 919) - *similar to disaster risk equation*
- Desire to protect property - whether from looters or the disaster itself.
- However, little link between length of residence and likelihood to evacuate - Backer (1991) suggests that potentially newer residents are more likely to evacuate because they are more likely to be moving into higher-risk areas (e.g., beachfront areas)
- Literature suggests a combination of risk characteristics:
	- (Perceived) severity of the threat
	- Amount of personal risk
	- Amount of family risk (and extending beyond to social community)
	- Territorial tendencies
	- Personal characteristics

## Social Influence and Norms

- People look to others to aid them with the decision-making process
- Norm: "the socially defined and enforced standard of behaviour that defines or limits the way a person should interpret the world, behave in it, or both (Deux et al., 1993)" (p. 921)
- During or the threat of disaster can cause a change in norms -> *links with [[Thomas and Mora, 2014]]*
- The greater one's connectivity to the community, the more likely they are to receive information to evacuation and follow it (Perry, 1979). Conversely, having a small social network is conducive to not evacuating (Drabek and Boggs, 1968)

## Access to Resources

- Perceived social support is considered a resource - lots of evacuees flee to homes of relatives rather than public shelters (Drabek and Boggs, 1968)
- Effect of prior experience - effects of previous experience on evacuation tendencies are mixed -> *coursework link: analyse former events in the region*

Study looks at 7777 adults interviewed after Hurricanes Hugo and Andrew
