# Three decades of landslide activity in western Nepal: new insights into trends and climate drivers


- Landslide disasters are increasing in the Himalayas
- Suggested link between increasing heavy rainfall under a warming climate
	- More intense monsoon [[Kitoh, 2017]]
- Also anthropogenic influences on slop stability - increases the exposure of people and assets to landslides
- Study creates a high-resolution landslide inventory to analyse frequency relationships and trends in relation to precipitation and temperature datasets.
- Results show a strong correlation between the annual number of shallow landslides and the accumulated monsoon precipitation.
- Warm and dry monsoons followed by especially rainy ones produce the highest incidence of shallow landslides 
- There is a strong spatial variability in the strength of these relationships - which has a link to recent demographic development in the region -> highlights the role of anthropogenic drivers:
	- Road cutting, land-use change
	- These amplify the seasonal monsoon influence on slope stability
- Study also identifies no long-term trend in landslide activity - therefore, despite reports of an increase in landslide disasters, this is not the case but rather signifies an increase in the exposure of people and infrastructure being the main driver of landslide disasters
