# Deciphering the effect of climate change on landslide activity: a review

|Change in climatic factor|Process affected|Effects on landslide response|
|---|---|---|
|Increased precipitation total|Wetter antecedent conditions  <br>Increased weight  <br>Higher water table for longer periods  <br>Increased river discharge|Less rainfall required to attain critical water content  <br>Reduction in soil suction and cohesion  <br>Increased shear stress  <br>Higher water table and reduction in shear strength  <br>Increased bulk density  <br>More frequent achievement of critical water content  <br>Increased bank erosion and removal of basal slope support  <br>Higher lake levels  <br>Higher coastal water tables  <br>Larger drawdown events and related drag forces|
|Increased rainfall intensity|Infiltration exceeds subsurface drainage  <br>Increased through flow  <br>Increased surface runoff|Build of perched water tables  <br>Reduction of effective normal stress  <br>Reduction in shear strength  <br>Increase seepage and drag forces  <br>Piping  <br>Increased surface erosion|
|Increase in air temperature|Higher evapotranspiration  <br>More abundant vegetation  <br>Higher hydraulic conductivity  <br>Rapid snowmelt  <br>Reduction in interstitial ice and permafrost|Reduction in antecedent water conditions  <br>More rainfall required to trigger landslides  <br>Higher evapotranspiration  <br>Reduced infiltration rate  <br>Higher root cohesion  <br>Higher infiltration  <br>Buildup of water tables, reduction of effective normal stress  <br>Higher runoff and infiltration  <br>Reduction in shear strength, reduction in cohesion in jointed rock masses  <br>Reduction in rock mass strength|
|Change in wind speed and duration|Enhanced evapotranspiration  <br>Enhanced root levering by trees  <br>Increased wave action on shorelines|Reduction in soil moisture  <br>Enhanced cracking, reduction of cohesion and soil strength  <br>Reduction of root cohesion  <br>Loosening and dislodging joint blocks  <br>Removal of slope lateral support|
|Change in weather systems|Areas previously unaffected (affected) subject to higher (lower) rainfall|Adjustment of slopes to changed weather conditions|
|Larger meteorological variability|More (less) frequent wetting and drying cycles|Increased fissuring  <br>Widening of joint systems  <br>Reduction of cohesion and rock mass friction|
