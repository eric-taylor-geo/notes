# Trenching studies of active faults in Kamchatka, eastern Russia: paleoseismic, tectonic and hazard implications


- The palaeorecord is insufficient to permit any robust correlation between Holocene faulting events and volcanic activity
- It may also be significant that all the faulting events are accompanied by eruptions but the opposite does not occur
- There may be some correlation between faulting events and the activity of the nearest volcanoes. In fact, all the faulting events are accompanied by eruptions, but the opposite does not occur. This correlation may reflect changes in stress fields at depth that squeeze (mafic) magma to the surface and cause brittle deformation in the crust.
