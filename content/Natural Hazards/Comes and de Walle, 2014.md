# Measuring Disaster Resilience: The Impact of Hurricane Sandy on Critical Infrastructure Systems

- Modern critical infrastructure (CI) systems are highly interconnected, making disruptions complex and difficult to predict, control, or limit.
- Disaster risk management requires a paradigm shift, moving from event prediction to enhancing resilience for better response to unforeseen events.
- The paper defines CI resilience to support both quantitative and qualitative decision-making.
- The quantitative model assesses the resilience of individual CIs, focusing on transportation and power supply, which are essential for disaster response.
- A qualitative framework examines the interconnections between different CIs.
- A case study on Hurricane Sandy demonstrates the need for a resilience framework integrating both qualitative and quantitative data from diverse sources.
