# Trends in landslide occurence in Nepal

- In Nepal, landslides represent a major constraint on development, causing high levels of economic loss and substantial numbers of fatalities each year.
- Three explanations are explored for this: land-use change, the effects of the ongoing civil war in Nepal, and road building. It is concluded that a major component of the generally upward trend in landslide impact probably results from the rural road-building programme, and its attendant changes to physical and natural systems.
## Road building:

- Expansion of Nepalese road network since 1990 to promote economic and social development within the country
	- Key emphasis on access for and to rural communities through the construction of an extensive road network on low technology rural roads.
- Lots of roads built are graven or earthen roads, using local human and physical resources as much as possible - thus, little engineering design input is put into their building - > high risk [[Sidle et al., 2006]]
- Road construction also changes the dynamics of local societies as they restructure to take advantage of the new opportunities presented by roads - can result in relocation to live beside roads - changes spatial exposure
